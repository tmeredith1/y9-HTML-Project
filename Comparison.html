SDG 6 & 14
What Do They Have In Common?
How are these two SDGs Related?

SDG 6 and 14 are truely connected by water.
Both have a goal of protecting sources of water so
that they can be used sustainably for many years to
come. Although there are obvious differences in the
missions of each SDG the common goal of protecting
humanity as well as ecosystems is significant. Along
with this both SDGs work in symbiotic relationship.
If rivers and waters are contaminated by feces
from those without proper sanitation, it can give
animals deseases as well as humans upon re-ingestion
of the fish. Both of these SDGs are trying to
accomplish an important mission and it is our goal
to help incourage those around us to be our best
possible selves.

Why is it Important we Focus on These
Two Causes?

Both SDG 6 and 14 are extremely significant causes
that can benefit our world in a number of ways.
Firstly, both benefit impoveriged nations. SDG 6 is
benefical as it supplies people with a lack
of clean drinking water with a clean source as well as
proper toilets, and soap. SDG 14 helps impovereged
nations by supplying them with jobs working on
boats or docks which many people have not
been able to maintain due to illegal
fisherman stealing catch. Illegal vessels stealing
catch has been a large problem and SDG 6 will work
to rectify that. Along with this, both SDGs benfit the
oceans as SDG 6 will reduce the amount of water contaminated by
humans and SDG 14 will improve the seas by increasing
animal life and biodiversity.

What can you do the Benefit Both
SDGs?

There are a number of ways you can support both
SDG 6 and 14. Firstly, donating to charities that
support the protection of oceans or clean drinking
water and sanitation. Although it may seem like you
are not really doing much, a small donation can go a
long way. Although there may not be as many ways
to actively impact SDG 6 there are certainly ways to
impact SDG 14. Firstly, try and buy fewer single use
plastics as they have little value and tend to end up in
oceans. If you see any plastics on the streets, try and
pick them up so they do not end up in a lake, river or
ocean. Although this seems small every bag off the
street could save the life of an marine animal.

What is so Significant About the Ocean?

Oceans are truely one of earth's greatest marvels.
No other planet in our solar system contains the beautiful
life bringing substance of water. It is extremely important
that we keep our oceans, lakes and rivers safe.
Oceans supply us with nearly 50 percent of our oxygen,
ensuring that our atmposphere stays supplied with
clean and breathable air. Along with this the ocean
supplies us with a great deal of our food. Truely,
the ocean is a lifeline for earth and for humanity and
it is our duty to keep it protected as
once it is gone it will never come back .
