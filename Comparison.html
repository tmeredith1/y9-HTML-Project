SDG 6 & 14
What Do They Have In Common?
How are these two SDGs Related?

SDG 6 and 14 are truly connected by water.
Both share the goal of protecting water sources so
that they can be used sustainably for many years to
come. Although there are obvious differences in the
missions of each SDG the common goal to protect
humanity as well as ecosystems is evident. Along
with this, both SDGs work in a symbiotic relationship.
If rivers and waters are contaminated by feces
from those without proper sanitation, it can give
both animals and humans diseases upon ingestion.
Both of these SDGs are trying to
accomplish an important mission and it is our goal
to help encourage those around us to be our best
possible selves.

Why is it Important we Focus on These
Two Causes?

Both SDG 6 and 14 are extremely significant goals
that can benefit our world in a number of ways.
Firstly, both benefit impoverished nations. SDG 6 is
benefical as it supplies people with a clean
water source as well as
proper toilets and soap. SDG 14 helps impoverished
nations by providing them with jobs
in the fishing industry. Furthermore, SDG 6 and 14
benfit the oceans as SDG 6 will reduce the
amount of water contaminated by humans and SDG 14
will improve the seas by increasing
animal life and biodiversity.

What can you do the Benefit Both
SDGs?

There are a number of ways you can support both
SDG 6 and 14. Donating to charities that
support the protection of oceans or clean drinking
water and sanitation is a first step. Although it may
seem like youare not really doing much, a small
donation can go a long way. Although there may not
be as many ways to actively impact SDG 6 there are
certainly ways to impact SDG 14. Try and buy fewer
single use plastics as they have little value and tend to
end up in oceans. If you see any plastic on the
streets, try and pick it up so they do not end up in
a lake, river orocean. Although this seems small
every bag off the street could save the life of a
marine animal.

What is so Significant About the Ocean?

Oceans are truly one of earth's greatest marvels.
No other planet in our solar system contains the beautiful
life bringing substance of water. It is extremely important
that we keep our oceans, lakes and rivers safe.
Oceans supply us with nearly 50 percent of our oxygen,
ensuring that our atmposphere stays supplied with
clean and breathable air. Along with this the ocean
supplies us with a great deal of our food.
The ocean is a lifeline for the earth and its humanity.
It is our duty to keep the waters protected as
once they are gone they will never come back .
