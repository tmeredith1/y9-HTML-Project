What is SDG 6?

SDG 6 is one of the United Nations 17 sustainable development
goals created in order to make our world the best place possible.
SDG 6 refers to securing clean, sustainable water and
sanitation for all. The UN hopes to accomplish the majority
of these goals so as to make the world a healthier and more
peaceful place by the year 2030. United Nation goals are ranked
from most to least important. Since SDG 6 is within the top 10
goals it therefore has significance to the world community. The
SDG 6 goal will be achieved by ensuring that people in low
infrustructure areas have both safe means to access water as well
as proper areas to sanitize and relieve themselves. The
achievement of SDG 6 is of the utmost importance
and every person supporting the cause
is an extremely significant player.

What is the Importance of SDG 6?

SDG 6 is of great significance to the world community.
Around the world, millions of people lack access to safely
managed water and sanitation services, along with
hand-washing facilities at home. This increases chances
of catching water born disease and in areas where medication
is scarce this can be deadly. It is evident that supporting SDG 6
is a matter of life or death for millions of people.
Without your support, the ability to aid people without access to
clean water and sanitation will take longer to achieve.
For example, in 2017 3 billion people lacked soap and water in
their homes, making it difficult to maintain positive hygene
practices.Without help from SDG 6, more and more people will
fall ill with diseases which could have been prevented by
strongerhygene measures. As you can see, there are a number of
reasons why your support will benefit SDG 6.

How Can You Help the Cause?

There are a number of ways that you can help the SDG 6
cause. Firstly, the United Nations outlines a number of
activites that you can participate in to improve the
livelihoods of those without clean water and proper
sanitation. These include forums, agenda reviews,
fundraisers and international celebrations. Along with this,
you can donate to a number of charities that work in the field
of clean water and sanitation such as Water for Good or
Generosity.Org. As you can see, there are a number of ways
you can be active in the fight to support SDG 6.

Facts about SDG 6

2.2 billion people lack safely managed drinking water
4.2 billion people lack safely managed sanitation.
3 billion people lack handwashing facilities at home.
2 out of 5 health clinics world wide do not have soap, water or
hand sanitizer.
2 billion people live in countries under high water stress.
Water scarcity could displace 700 million people by 2030.
